Career timeline

  • Software engineering (early career)

    Why this choice: I wanted to build things end‑to‑end and learn the craft of software by shipping to real users.

    Work: Full‑stack engineering across web and backend; focused on reliability, correctness, and shipping value quickly.

    Why this choice: I believed that working on foundational learning is one of the most leveraged ways to create opportunity.

    What I learned: How systems, incentives, and constraints shape outcomes; empathy; and designing for real‑world constraints.

    Why I moved: I wanted to combine social impact with technology at scale — toward AI and public‑interest systems.

  • Founder — Samta.Law

    Why this choice: Legal information is fragmented and hard to access; retrieval‑augmented AI could lower that barrier.

    Work: Built a GenAI‑powered legal search engine from zero to acquisition at an early stage.

    What I learned: Operating production LLM systems, retrieval quality, evaluation, and the realities of zero‑to‑one product building.

    Why I moved: To apply these learnings at platform scale and contribute to India’s AI and digital public infrastructure efforts.

  • Founding member — Sarvam AI

    Why this choice: Build production‑grade AI systems and platforms that are locally relevant and globally competitive.

    Work: LLM systems, conversational agents, and platform capabilities used in real‑world policy and inclusion contexts.

    What I’m learning: Building resilient AI products, safety and evaluation at scale, and ecosystem design for public‑interest tech.

    What’s next: Continue to build durable, useful AI systems and strengthen open, consented digital ecosystems.
